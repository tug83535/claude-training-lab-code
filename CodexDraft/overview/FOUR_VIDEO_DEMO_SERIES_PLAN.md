# Four-Video Demo Series Plan (5-10 Minutes Each)

## Context
You already created a first version with **4 short demos** (5-10 minutes each). That is a strong format.

This plan turns that into a polished series with clear progression for coworkers and leadership.

---

## Recommended 4-Video Structure

## Video 1 — "Excel/VBA: Fast Workflow in Familiar UI" (5-8 min)
**Goal:** Show immediate usability for finance users.

- Open Command Center
- Run 2-3 high-value macro actions
- Show before/after in workbook
- Close with: "No coding required for daily use"

**Win condition:** viewer sees speed and ease in Excel.

## Video 2 — "SQL: Data Trust and Reconciliation" (6-10 min)
**Goal:** Prove accuracy/control layer.

- Show raw vs staged/normalized concept
- Demonstrate reconciliation/exception logic
- Show one audit-style output (exceptions, dedup, rule check)

**Win condition:** viewer sees this is not just formatting; it improves data integrity.

## Video 3 — "Python: Forecasting + Executive Outputs" (6-10 min)
**Goal:** Show advanced analytics beyond native spreadsheet workflows.

- Run one forecast/simulation flow
- Show KPI/variance outputs
- Generate one leadership-ready artifact (chart/report)

**Win condition:** viewer sees strategic insight value, not just automation.

## Video 4 — "End-to-End Close Story" (8-10 min)
**Goal:** Tie all three technologies into one business narrative.

- Start with messy monthly inputs
- Execute VBA -> SQL -> Python flow
- End with reconciled, explainable, presentation-ready outcome

**Win condition:** viewer sees a repeatable enterprise workflow.

---

## Episode Design Rules (Important)

1. One business question per video (avoid feature dumps).
2. Show measurable outcome (time saved, errors caught, confidence gained).
3. Keep each episode independently understandable.
4. Reuse the same sample company/data theme to build continuity.
5. End each video with "what this means for our finance team." 

---

## Suggested KPI Overlay for Each Video

- **Speed:** manual time vs automated time
- **Quality:** error checks or exceptions caught
- **Control:** what was logged/audited
- **Decision support:** what insight leadership gets

If a demo cannot show at least two of the above, revise it.

---

## Non-Duplication Check (M365 vs Custom Value)
Before finalizing each episode, confirm:
- Is this something native Excel/Power Query can already do in 2-3 clicks?
- If yes, either remove it or pair it with audit/control logic that native flow does not enforce.

---

## Practical Recording Sequence

1. Record Video 1 and Video 2 first (foundation + trust).
2. Record Video 3 third (advanced value).
3. Record Video 4 last (integrated story) once all pieces are stable.
4. Keep each raw recording under 12 minutes; trim to 5-10 final.

---

## Recommendation for Your Current Version
Since you already have 4 videos, the next best step is **re-cut and standardize**:
- Add a 10-second opening frame with episode title + business question.
- Add a 20-second closing frame with outcomes (speed/quality/control).
- Keep visual style consistent across all four.

This keeps your current work and upgrades it to leadership-ready quality quickly.
