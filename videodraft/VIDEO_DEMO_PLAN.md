## The Big Picture — What We Are Trying to Accomplish

This is not a training video. This is a **sales pitch to your own company**. You are showing 2,000+ people — including the CEO and CFO — that one Finance team member built something that saves the department hundreds of hours per year, at zero cost, using only Excel and free tools.

The video needs to accomplish three things:
1. **Make leadership proud** — "This is the kind of innovation we want at the company"
2. **Make coworkers excited** — "I want to use this. How do I get it?"
3. **Make it look easy** — "I don't need to be technical to use this"

## Video Format Options — Pros and Cons

### Option A: Three Short Videos (6-7 min each)
**Pros:**
- People actually watch short videos — attention drops after 7 minutes
- Leadership can watch just Part 1 and get the idea
- Easier to reshoot one bad section without redoing everything
- Can be shared individually ("Just watch Part 2 if you do month-end close")

**Cons:**
- More editing work (3 intros, 3 outros)
- Viewers might not watch all 3

### Option B: One Full Video (18-22 min)
**Pros:**
- Simpler to record — one take, one upload
- Tells the full story in one sitting

**Cons:**
- 20 minutes is long for corporate video — many people will skip or stop halfway
- If you flub something at minute 15, you may have to redo everything

### Option C: Hybrid — One Full + Chapters (RECOMMENDED)
**Pros:**
- Record it as one continuous video
- Add **chapter markers** (YouTube, SharePoint, and most video players support this)
- Viewers can jump to the section they care about
- Leadership watches the first 5 minutes, power users watch the whole thing

**How:**
- Record one smooth 18-20 minute video
- In post, add chapter timestamps:
  - 0:00 — Introduction
  - 1:30 — Command Center Overview
  - 4:30 — Data Quality & Letter Grade
  - 6:00 — Reconciliation
  - 8:00 — Variance Analysis & Auto Commentary
  - 11:00 — Dashboard & PDF Export
  - 13:00 — Version Control & Scenarios
  - 16:00 — Integration Tests & Wrap-Up

## Ideas to Make the Video Stand Out

### 1. "Before vs After" Split Screen
At the start or end, show a quick comparison:
- **Left side:** "Before" — scattered Excel files, manual reconciliation, hours of formatting
- **Right side:** "After" — one file, Command Center, 10-second reconciliation

This could be as simple as two screenshots side by side with text labels. Takes 15 seconds but makes the point immediately.

### 2. Timer on Screen
While running key actions (reconciliation, variance analysis), show a real-time counter in the corner. When it hits "10 seconds" and the work is done, the impact is visceral.

### 3. Start with the Pain
Open with a 30-second story about the old way:
> "Every month, we spend [X] hours pulling GL data, reconciling spreadsheets, building reports, and writing variance commentary. What if I told you all of that can be done in under 10 minutes?"

Then immediately demonstrate it.

### 4. End with the Number
Close on a single screen that says something like:
> "62 actions. 0 cost. 10 minutes vs 10 hours."

Simple. Memorable. Shareable.

### 5. Show the Disclaimer Sheet
Quick 5-second flash showing the Disclaimer sheet — reinforces professionalism and that the data is demo-only. Shows you thought of everything.

### 6. Show Real Speed
Do NOT edit out the processing time. Let the viewer see the action run in real time. If reconciliation takes 8 seconds, show all 8 seconds. The speed IS the feature.

### 7. Music (Optional)
Very soft, corporate background music during the opening and closing only. No music during the live demo — your voice and Excel should be the focus. Free corporate music available from YouTube Audio Library.

## Script Approaches — Three Styles

### Style 1: Casual Walkthrough (Recommended)
- Talk naturally, like showing a coworker something cool
- "Hey, let me show you what this does..."
- Feels authentic, relatable, not rehearsed
- Best for: Building excitement and making people feel comfortable

### Style 2: Formal Presentation
- Structured, professional narration
- "Today I will demonstrate the P&L Automation Toolkit..."
- Best for: CFO/CEO meetings, formal rollouts

### Style 3: Problem-Solution Demo
- Start with the problem, immediately solve it
- "Right now, reconciliation takes us 2 hours. Watch this. [runs action] Done. 10 seconds."
- Best for: Maximum impact, short attention spans

**Recommendation:** Style 1 for the full video, with Style 3 energy during the key demo moments.

## Practical Recording Tips

### Things That Kill Corporate Demo Videos
1. **Too much talking before showing** — show first, explain second
2. **Reading from a script word-for-word** — sounds robotic. Know your bullet points, talk naturally
3. **Screen too zoomed out** — viewers watch on laptops/phones. 1920x1080, Excel at 100% zoom minimum
4. **Background noise** — USB mic or headset. Test before recording
5. **Notifications popping up** — turn off Teams, Outlook, Windows notifications, phone
6. **Rushing** — pause after each action for 2-3 seconds. Let the result sink in
7. **No energy** — if you're bored, the viewer is bored. Be genuinely excited (you should be — this is impressive)

### Things That Make Corporate Demo Videos Great
1. **First 15 seconds grab attention** — don't start with "Hi, today we're going to..."
2. **Show, don't tell** — minimize slides, maximize live Excel
3. **Call out the time savings** — "That used to take 2 hours. We just did it in 10 seconds."
4. **Let silence work** — after a big result, pause for 2 seconds. Let them absorb it.
5. **End with a clear CTA** — "The file is on SharePoint. Open it and try Action 7. That's all you need to do."

## Flow Recommendations

### The "10-Second Rule"
For each feature you demo, the viewer should understand what it does within 10 seconds of seeing it. If you're still explaining after 10 seconds, it's too complex for video. Simplify or skip.

### The "So What?" Test
After every demo, answer: "So what? Why does this matter to the person watching?"
- Reconciliation passes → "You can tell your manager the numbers tie. With proof."
- Letter grade A → "You know the data is clean before you start working on it."
- Auto commentary → "Your variance write-up is done before you finish your coffee."

### Suggested Flow
1. **Hook** (0:00-0:30) — "What if I told you we can do month-end close in 10 minutes?"
2. **Context** (0:30-1:30) — Quick tour of the workbook, show it's one file
3. **Command Center** (1:30-3:00) — Open it, show the search, show the 62 actions
4. **Data Quality** (3:00-5:00) — Run scan, show letter grade (first wow moment)
5. **Reconciliation** (5:00-6:30) — Run checks, show PASS/FAIL (time savings moment)
6. **Variance** (6:30-9:00) — Run analysis + commentary (biggest wow moment)
7. **Visuals** (9:00-11:00) — Dashboard + PDF export (deliverable moment)
8. **Advanced** (11:00-15:00) — Version control, sensitivity, scenarios, exec mode
9. **Reliability** (15:00-16:30) — Integration test, 18/18 PASS
10. **Close** (16:30-18:00) — Recap numbers, where to get the file, CTA
